Classes 課程

From beginners to disciples — traditional Wu Style Tai Chi Chuan taught at every level.

Form 表格

Begin with the 12-step form, then progress through the 108-step small and large circle forms. Taught one-on-one or in small groups so you move at your own pace.

Pushing Hands 推手

Two-person exercises from basic to complex 9-palace form. Also includes Chi Kung and form refinements for intermediate students.

Weapon Forms 武器型態

Spear, Sabre, and Sword — offered to advanced intermediate students in that order, building on the foundation of the hand form.

Disciples 門徒

Full members of the Academy with access to open training, advanced form refinement, Chi Kung, applications, and teaching Tai Chi. Formally accepted by the Shifu (师父) into the school's lineage.

5 Steps / Ba Fa Wu Bu 太极八法五步

Clears the body's meridians, balances qi and blood, and eases chronic pain. Suitable for all ages — a gateway to personal peace and tranquility.

Our School History 我們的校史

Master Wu Tai Chi conducting a workshop at Old Detroit School location

Our school has been serving the Metro Detroit area for over 30 years — working with seniors and hospital patients across Southeast Michigan, teenagers through Cranbrook's Horizons Upward Bound program, and a wide range of everyday people at 24 satellite locations and our main Academy.

In 1935, the first Wu's Tai Chi Chuan Academy was founded in Shanghai by Grandmaster Wu Chien-ch'uan. Our instructors are certified at Advanced Level by the International Wu Style Tai Chi Chuan Federation.

"The 108 posture empty hand form routine is the essence. Regular practice strengthens bones and muscles… Preventing disease and prolonging life, Tai Chi Chuan nourishes one's vitality.

Two person pushing hands is the application. The methodology follows the Tai Chi principle of movement and stillness. Applications are expressed through the subtle transformations that occur between fullness and emptiness. Applications use the principles of psychology and physics. Softness overcomes hardness, and hardness reinforces softness." — Grandmaster Wu Kung-tsao

Notable Disciples

Wu Quanyou

Founder of Wu Style. Military officer and disciple of Yang Luchan — laid the foundations of the style's internal martial art.

Wu Jianquan

Co-founder of Wu Style. Taught large numbers and refined the art to distinguish it from Yang style.

Wu Gongyi

Moved family headquarters to Hong Kong in 1948. Senior instructor from 1942 to 1970.

Wu Ta-k'uei

Late grand master credited with promoting Tai Chi in Canada and internationally.

Eddie Wu Guangyu

Current grandmaster. Great-grandson of Wu Jianquan and grandson of Wu Gongyi.
